Associate Professor

Cecilia Ovesdotter Alm, Ph.D.

Cecilia's research interests focus on linguistic sensing and language-inclusive sensing with text, speech, and multimodal data. She has special interests in meaning, affect, multimodality, health/wellbeing, and education, and is dedicated to supporting student research and training experiences. Cecilia is the PI of an NSF award entitled REU Site: Computational Sensing. She is often involved in interdisciplinary research collaborations. Besides her Ph.D. in Linguistics from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (centered on computational linguistics), she also holds a Certificate of Advanced Study in Language and Speech Processing and a Certificate of Graduate Specialization in Computational Science and Engineering from UIUC. Link to CV.

Assistant Professor

Emily Prud'hommeaux, Ph.D.

Emily Prud'hommeaux's research focuses on applications of natural language processing and speech signal processing in the medical domain, particularly for identifying linguistic markers of neurological disorders such as autism and dementia. In addition, she maintains research interests in language modeling for speech recognition and in applications of word alignment beyond machine translation. She received her PhD in Computer Science from OHSU, an MA in Linguistics from UCLA, and an AB in Linguistics from Harvard. Between degrees, she worked for several years in the software industry at two start-ups and at Nuance, where she was a member of the language modeling R&D group. Link to CV
