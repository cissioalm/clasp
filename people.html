Assistant Professor

Emily Prud'hommeaux, Ph.D.

Emily Prud'hommeaux's research focuses on applications of natural language processing and speech signal processing in the medical domain, particularly for identifying linguistic markers of neurological disorders such as autism and dementia. In addition, she maintains research interests in language modeling for speech recognition and in applications of word alignment beyond machine translation. She received her PhD in Computer Science from OHSU, an MA in Linguistics from UCLA, and an AB in Linguistics from Harvard. Between degrees, she worked for several years in the software industry at two start-ups and at Nuance, where she was a member of the language modeling R&D group. Link to CV
